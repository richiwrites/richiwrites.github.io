Mental Health

Health May 29,2020

richi.writes It's now more important than ever to talk about mental health considering the testing phase we all are going through. I talked to my mom who works with mental health and learnt a lot. It's great that so many people worldwide are talking about mental health, normalising mental illness and sharing their experiences. But we still have a long way to go because even now seeking clinical help due to mental health issues is avoided as much as ... possible. People often think mental health issues depend on mood or "it's all in our head" and it will go away if we decide to ignore it. That's mostly because mental health problems are not easily understandable unlike our physical illnesses.

But ignoring any disease is NOT the cure to it. It impacts our thoughts, emotions, beliefs, our whole inner state. Mental health is just as important as physical health, if not more. It's unhealthy and risky to live with the unimaginable sense of emptiness a brain can feel. Our brain is a vital organ of our body and it also deserves proper treatment just like the other organs. Suffering from mental health issues is not a matter to be hidden, not something to be ashamed of. It's completely okay to eliminate the intangible stress that affects our healthy lifestyle. It DOES NOT go away if we push it, we do not just "forget it", it is mundane and it exists. It needs to be addressed and treated.

We need to trust the process of healing and we're not going to let it slide. Chronic depression, acute anxiety, all these are medical terms and there are professionals to help us get through this. So we don't just "brush it off". We grow and outgrow, we FOCUS on our mental health, we assess and reassess our problems, we take help and we get better. We steer towards healing before it leads to other hydra headed problems. There are people who have suffered, reached out for help and have overcome this phase. I would tell all of you to open up and inspire the others going through similar problems. Let's talk about it, share our experiences and seek help if required. These are NOT the stories of our "problems" or weakness, these are only our STORIES OF STRENGTH.

Black Lives Matters

Equality June 3,2020

richi.writes After yet another brutal death of a black man, there has erupted a global protest. Today, we call for a revolution. Today we ask for the privileged voices to be muted for the muted minority voices to be heard. And the movement in America is not an indication for a war, the objective is to stop the racism war going on for decades for once and for all.
... I can put million reasonings as to why racism should stop. But the enormity of racism has been a part of the vast history of colonial subjugation and oppression. History shows harrowing deaths of innocent black people, the rate of black death by police is higher than any other minority group. Enough of trying to change people's views and treatment through thoughtful insights. Now it's time to WORK and take ACTIONS to actually contribute to the uprising. Because black people face injustice in every sphere of their life every single day. We are only voicing our opinions when their deaths unfold in front of our eyes, it's only now we are aware how brutal the situation in reality is.

Racism is deep rooted. We need to speak up and act against it wherever we encounter the smallest act of race deprivation. THAT is the motive of the movement. It all starts with us. So what we need to do is PAUSE. We do our part. We EDUCATE. REFLECT. READ. LEARN. LISTEN. Donate and sign petitions. Listen to what the black activists are portraying in their anti racist movements. This is for Eric Garner, Sandra Bland, George Floyd, for Black liberation. For black culture, black music, black lives. Because BLACK LIVES MATTER.

White privilege exists. Racism exists. Black mothers have felt the death of George Floyd in their motherhood and Black people have now been pushed to a point to risk it all to scream for justice, to call for a revolution.

Let's involve with organizations to donate, sign petitions and do everything in our power to help George Floyd recieve justice for I'm sure he is looking forward to a change in this world from up above.

Let's work so we never have to see another human being strangled to death on streets, gasping for breath and begging to survive.

Superwomen

Womenpower June 8,2020

richi.writes
I browse the internet and i come across an article that reads

A woman turns into a superwoman on the seven days she bleeds

Menstruation is not an achievement, our body... is constructed this way

But what makes us superwomen is the unbearable cramps on a period day

On the days my legs refuse to walk and my hips feel like being ripped off

You can never really tell because you've never watched me stop

I don't stop because I still manage to run to catch my bus so I don't run late

We, as women, have accepted the pain of period considering this as our fate

You might now say, "com'on girl stop whining it's not that big of a deal

We've tweeted and posted on social media we all know the drill"

Yes you know about period but do you know about dysmenorrhea, a disease?

It's the medical term of menstrual cramps caused due to the uterine squeeze

Oligomennorhea, referring to missed menstruation for more than 35 days

These can lead to severe gynocologic disorders, if not addressed, as research says

So it's not something that women born before you have suffered now you must too

It might be a disease that needs to be cured, don't just silently "go through"

As estrogen secreting humans, we face a whole week we flow a red sea

Stuck inside a heavy cotton pad, the female organ wishes there was a better place to be

Our medicines wrapped in transparent polythenes but pads, in a black one to hide

We, the "superwomen", have still not learnt how to bleed with pride "Whisper", "freedom", "stayfree" all bought for our red stains to be invisibilized

Women in remote villages, still using clothes, where menstruation is still not normalized

Dear men, please talk about period to your daughter and your wife

Because an ignorance might even risk their life

You see me calm, quiet and focused, working at my desk

You cannot really tell my cramps are killing me, even surviving feels like a test

Despite the pain and sufferings, I'm proud of the blood I flow

Because I fight wars with my body so that in my womb, a new human can grow

Flowing a red ocean every month with this pain, it's not easy, you see

But if God gives me the power, a woman is all I'll ever want to be.
