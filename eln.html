Mother's Day May 9,2021

richi.writes
You say mothers don't need a separate day for appreciation
You say every day is a mother's Day
I say it's not.
I say every day is anything but mother's Day

Because everytime mom tells us to keep ... the phone aside and sit together for dinner, we don't listen.
We forget how in childhood we cried our hearts out to be fed by her.
Everytime we're out with friends and mom keeps calling, we put the phone on silent.
We forget how many times we used to call mom only to irritate her in work. Everytime mom asks us to reply to a Facebook comment, we get irritated "Why can't you do this yourself?"
We forget who held our fingers the first time we wrote ABCD

No that's not all.

There are single mothers struggling for survival fencing their children from the cruelty of the world.
There are single fathers having no clue about how to raise children yet buying pads for their daughters and making them tea to help soothe the cramps.
There are moms-to-be with medical complications, unsure if the child will even be born.
Moms-to-be who aren't married, abandoned by the whole society.
There are mothers who are handed over their stillborn child.
There are mothers who have had a miscarriage.
There are mothers who have been stars on the sky way too early.
Mothers who just got to know they'll never be able to give birth.
Mothers who adopted children to provide them with the warmth of a family.
There are mothers who work and can't attend their children's important events.
There are mothers who resign from jobs.
There are mothers of every kind.
And no, we do not celebrate mothers the way they deserve to be celebrated.

It's okay we're a generation of shy kids
Who'll probably never tell our mom how much warmth it gives us when we wake up shivering in cold at midnights and there are two hands putting a blanket on us.

It's okay to have a day for mothers
A day for every kind of mother out there.
You don't have to be superheroes.
God made you a mother. That's more than enough.
