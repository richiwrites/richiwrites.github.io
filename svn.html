Freedom

Justice October 11,2020

richi.writes The past few days have been draining. All the news and events were terrifying and even though it's nothing new, it once again grew a sense of terror in me. I no longer feel safe in my own country. I cry for women around the world who are vulnerable and get no justice after the tortures they've been through. I cry for women altogether. When I sit and reflect, I am angry and I strongly despise how there are complete bullshits being thrown at women. And sexual harassment, trust me when I say this, almost every girl in our country... has faced this one way or the other EVERY SINGLE DAY she walks out. I've been watching documentaries and listening to women sharing their stories of sexual harassment and this terrifies me because every girl could relate.

It's all been so heavy to carry. So today I decided to sit and think. I'm a woman and I realize that if I have to survive, I have to fight. This war is constant. But if you want to take some rest, if you want to give yourself a break, it's OKAY. It's totally okay if you want your cup of tea and reread an old book and all you do is survive the day. You deserve a break. It can be too draining. After all, a human brain can cope with just so much.

So I decided to read books, bask in the warmth the sun has to offer and just do nothing. Because sometimes, that's what we all need. To breathe throughout a day. I gave myself that. I want you to give it to yourself too. But remember, at the end, you got to come back and fight. It's now more important than ever to speak up, to fight, to scream, to stand against evil, to stand for the good. Take some rest. When you come back, we'll fight together.

Didi

Part of me October 30,2020

richi.writes I lost one of the most important persons in my life on the last 24th October. I believe even to say I lost my life would be an understatement. I lost my Didi. I lost another mother. I lost everything I held onto. My Didi was my caretaker since my birth as both my parents are working. She brought me up, she was my shadow. There is not a single person who knows me but doesn't know my Didi. She has always been everywhere I've been. This Puja, she went on a vacation to her own village, met with... a road accident and passed away (may her soul rest in peace).

She left me crying like a maniac, shaking terribly, not having a clue about what's going on and wailing. I had no idea what to do. I bade her the grandest goodbye. I cried and screamed so much the whole hospital stopped to see. They all cried with me for her. I shouted I love her cause we never told each other this before. I never spent a single second without her. All I learnt, I learnt from her after all. Living without her is not one of the things she taught. She was there when I took my first step, when I uttered my first word, my first day at school, college and every single little or big event in my life. She was (still is, I believe) so proud of me. Even when I failed. Especially when I failed. I miss seeing the pride in her eyes when I did the littlest good thing. I miss her with every beat of my heart. Winter

Seasonal Love November 30,2020

richi.writes Winter! Oh, how I love winter! Winter for me has been about warmth and colours and that festive vibe all around Dhaka, the wedding lightings, colorful sweaters and that first touch of cold water on my skin in the morning, touching friends with frozen hands and roadside teas. This was before Corona punched us all in the guts and derided festivity with an evil smirk. This winter for me is a bit unsettling considering the fact that I'll probably be on my bed all day with a physics book on my side, abandoned, a laptop, a phone,... a cup of tea and one mess of a room. This lifestyle in my pre Corona life sounded like a luxury mostly because I couldn't afford it even if I wanted to. But now when all I have is sleep and tons of piled up work, I miss every single thing about December in Dhaka.

Regardless, I don't think anything can ever underscore my love for winters. This year, I have experienced the highest level of grief, been away from my friends and my college campus that I can't help complaining about and those familiar sector 6 roads that feel like home. But the tipping point for me was when winter finally arrived and I'm still locked within my four walls. Nevertheless I welcome winter with wide and warm hands, hoping for some warmth in my oversized sweatshirts, searching for some familiar love in my hundredth cup of tea. Even though I can't be on my beloved Dhaka roads in winter stuffing myself with fritters and roadside tea, rubbing my hands against each other, I'll try to celebrate this winter in covid way. Which includes opting for some undone assignments and studying for tests instead of walking in the fog. Winter, with all its festive magics and warm hugs is now firmly history, the nostalgia of yesteryears.

This winter is markedly different. I procrastinate, I sleep all day all night and I'm not getting things done at all. But I know I need to. I'll have to do my maths, study for my biology tests, sometimes join my online classes and tick at least some boxes if not all. But that's okay. The winter sun will shine, my grip will be firm against my hot cup of tea and things will feel okay. Wish y'all a warm Covid winter with its own different magic!!
