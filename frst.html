সন্ধ্যেবেলা

Poem May 14,2020

richi.writes Wrote a bangla poem after ages my friends forced me into posting im embarrassed

নিয়ম করে সন্ধ্যে নেমেছে আজও
ঠিক যেমনটা সন্ধ্যে নামত আগে
শ্রাবণ সন্ধ্যে ...একই রকম আছে
তুমি শুধু নেই চেনা পথের বাকেঁ. শহর জুড়ে সন্ধে নামে যখন
মেঘও যখন আসছি আসছি করে
সোডিয়াম লাইট আলোর মিছিলে নামে
তুমিহীন আমি ডুবছি অন্ধকারেই

তোমার আমার সুখের মুহূর্তরা
বন্দী এখন ডায়রি পাতায় পাতায়
কবিতা থাক তোমার জন্য আজও
গল্পেরা থাক আমার খসড়া খাতায়
তোমার আমার শ্রাবণ সন্ধেগুলো
হারিয়েছে শেষে সময়ের গতি বেগে
খুজেঁ দেখ তোমার এলোমেলো পাঞ্জাবি
সেখানে আমার শাড়ির সুতোরা লেগে

বর্ষা বিকেলে তুমিহীন আমি যখন
স্বস্তি খুঁজছি শান্ত কফির কাপে
এই শহরও আমারই মত তখন
গা ভাসাচ্ছে ভীষণ নিম্নচাপে

সোডিয়াম আলোয় ভেজা রাস্তারা জানে
একই বৃষ্টিতে ভিজেছে একদিন যারা
তাদের ভেতর দুরত্ব আজ অনেক
নেই আজ তাদের বৃষ্টি ভেজার তাড়া

সন্ধ্যেবেলায় আলোর মিছিল এই শহরে
কালো পিচের রাস্তারা ভেজে জলে
ভিড়ের মাঝে মিশতে থাকে পায়ের জোড়া
নতুন প্রেমের গল্প তারা বলে

এম্নি যখন নামবে শ্রাবণ সন্ধেবেলা
নাইবা হলো নেশা তোমাকে ছুঁতে চাওয়ার
কফির কাপের শেষ চুমুকে অপেক্ষা থাক
তোমাকে শুধু আরেকটাবার দেখতে পাওয়ার.

Equality

Voice May 17,2020

richi.writes Today I came across a harrowing incident which got me thinking for a long while-what did we girls do wrong to deserve all the maltreatments thrown at us every single day?

There are a lot of poignant cases of slut shaming and body shaming and using the worst words possible to describe us women, objectifying us for "fun" happening in a large scale every... single day. And it's just a sordid mentality to normalize the defaming of women claiming it to be "not that serious". Honestly, it puts my heart at ease to see people taking stands, talking about these, using their voices all loud and clear to fight these distorted mindsets. And it gives me a strength for I have seen men being vocal about these terrifying events and it's for the sane enough ideologies of these men why we still reach out for their help in this context. It's 2020 for God's sake and we women are still asking you all to be a little kinder to us. We have foresaken the idea of equality already and all we want now is to survive in a bit safer society where we get a 'little' more respect because to expect the amount of respect we deserve is a wishful thinking. We can count the very little number of men we can trust and we're scared of the rest. We do not want to be just another rape victim, right? How long will we be degraded, disrespected and scrutinized simply for being women? It's 2020, the "modern" era, and we are still begging to be considered less "female" (or should I say 'objects') and more "human".

Objectifying humans just out of rage

If defaming... women is a crime then so is degrading men
Men are NOT dogs and it's time we understand
A 'human' deserves respect
not a 'woman' or a 'man'.
So let's end this debate and get this straight.

Men also have nothing to prove
It's time for us to disapprove
All the ideas we've been taught
For men too have fought

Because

Quarantine

Positivity May 23,2020

richi.writes If I'm being honest, I'm actually scared. With a virus claiming thousands of lives everyday and a pandemic breaking out, we all are going through the toughest time with loads of questions having no answers, uncertainties looming around and fear of the unknowns. One thing I have realized is the only thing that's guaranteed in life is death.

If we're surviving without having to worry about our next meal, we're standing at a position of privilege. Corona is not what we can control, but the least we can do is help the ...underprivileged while we still can. I would entreat all of you to donate as much as you can and this will be of great help during this testing time.

The prolonged lockdown has instilled fatigue and frustrations into us. Our mental health, just like the world economy, is falling down. Being confined at home for 67 days has taught me a lot of things but mostly the importance of empathy and gratefulness. This pandemic feels like a wake-up call to remind us how short and uncertain this life is . My school uniform, school bag are set aside as the constant reminder of this being the new normal. It's not easy to adapt to this but it surprises me how quickly we have adjusted with this life. Humans are truly amazing.

I have realized that spreading positivity proves to be a good coping mechanism. I have been trying to spread some extra love, extra kindness around. Internet surely is a huge blessing in this difficult phase where time is at a standstill. We can connect to the people we miss, online classes is the new normal (I HATE THEM) and even though nothing feels the same anymore, some words of appreciation, little sweet compliments can really prove to be effective enough to lessen the stress to some extent.

And I'm sure we will come out of this as much better versions of ourselves for we have faced mortality fear and realized how important every little moment in life is. In this life altering situation, I would tell you all to just spread some extra kindness through internet and let's try to set our love free from our four walled rooms.
