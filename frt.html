Our favourite outfits left forgotten when we reach our teens
We were told not to wear them, they show too much of our skin
We wear our tops high, with legs closed we sit
We don't want them thinking we ...are "asking for it"

For your nasty comments and shaking of heads
Because I couldn't match the "good girl" image you've set
I'm taught in my books to always keep my head high
It's you who taught me I should lower my eye
I learn from my teachers men and women have equal rights
I learn from you to stay silent while I'm teased in broad daylight
You pay for neither my makeup, nor my dress
Yet you comment my makeup is too much, my clothes too less
You taught me to be scared of "what will people say"
Your nasty comments resist me from living my life my way
Before you judge girls on streets, check on your sons first
So that no girl like us becomes a prey to his lust
We're told it's normal to be teased on streets
And we're told to stay silent, to just "ignore it"
So we continue being teased in schools, streets and malls
We're the ones to protect our family's honour, after all
This is to remind you, dearest aunty My body belongs to NOONE but me
Not the uncle that touched me wrong in the bus
I endured because I was taught to not create a fuss
Not the neighbourhood boys and their lecherous eyes
I'm the one to own my body and I won't let you tell me otherwise
Next time their eyes linger on my body, don't be surprised
Because I will make sure I uproot those filthy eyes
We WON'T change our clothing, let that sink in
What really needs to change is the way you think
The way I choose to dress might even risk my life
And I won't say I could be their sister, mother or wife
Because they do not need to be related to me
I want to be respected because, yes, I deserve to be
And next time, we will turn around and slap these men on face
Won't let aunties tell us we're the family's disgrace
My body is NOT their territory and certainly not my shame
If someone touches me wrong, I'm not the one to blame
From this day onwards, our dignity WE will uphold
Being silent clearly hasn't worked, from today we'll raise our voice BOLD

My Competitions

Life goes on June 28,2020

richi.writes
I don't have any medal or trophy. My achievements are confined in one coin that I won for a reciation when I was 8 or so. And if I exclude the participation certificates in different fests, I

...literally have nothing to show off when guests come home. . When I was in play group, I somehow ended up being a participant in the annual sports. Ma was probably out of town so my aunt took me. As the whistle blew, everyone ran and guess who stood still. My aunt kept shouting "Run! Run!" and I literally said, "ora age jak" (let them go first). Here goes my sport skills and also, my common sense. Then my parents took me for singing class and the teacher was apparently very fond of taking "sa re ga ma pa" exams and I, obviously, didn't realize I should memorize it. And the "aaaaaa" of the teacher scared me so I quit after 3 days (and now whoever listens to me singing ends up with bleeding ears). Then my parents got me admitted into an art school. Needless to mention, I sucked at art. Then I quit the art school and went to the art teacher at my school to just learn the arts that would appear on my eigth standard boards question. And soon, I was over with my boards along with my art.

. Then I used to debate and I remember I even won once. But then, it kind of stopped. Didn't quit that intentionally, it just didn't happen anymore. Then I joined the 'film and drama club' back when my school used to arrange "club days" on saturdays. I really liked that but then suddenly, no club days anymore. I did one MUN and wanted to attend more but I did a terrible result in my recent exams and guess the rest.

. It actually makes me laugh while documenting all this that I never stuck to anything. Other than just opening a writing account because I suddenly felt like it and posting pinterest pictures (now sahaba makes me illustrations huhu) with lengthy bullshits as captions. But truth be told, it's okay I guess. It's okay to suck at extra curricular activities (grapes are sour) AND academics as well. Fine, let's just admit I have no clue where I'm going with my life. And as far as it's about medals, com'on, I'm the cutest medal around my neck. Told you, I never make sense so why bother.

Happiness

Looking forward July 2,2020

richi.writes
While I'm finally well after a whole night of no sleep and continuous vomitting and an unbearable stomachache (thank you, polao mangsho), I'm just allowed "healthy" soup and I kindof like it(?). Few sips of my evening tea today brought back a refreshment that the food-poisoned Richita was missing. And while my last post was about the arts I could never master, the "healthy" soup gave my brain the idea to think about my achievements. As I think about my wins, weirdly enough, they mount higher ...than my never-perfected arts.

. While I'm writing this, I'm reminiscing a beautiful experience of 2019. There was a talent show at my school and I went to audition for anchoring mostly to bunk classes. Only one student was to be selected because the other pair had already been selected. We all auditioned and I couldn't believe my well functioning ears when the teacher selected ME. I was assigned to write the script. I had a wonderful team to work with. We didn't rehearse even once. 1 hour before performance, we just went through the script and that's it. But when I held the mic and started speaking, I felt so comfortable even though I was completely new to this. We enjoyed so much and when it ended with applauses, I was complemented by so many people and it still feels like a miraculous dream too good to be true. I was actually being appreciated for something I did for the very first time (?)

. Then this account that I opened as a spiteful decision. And here I met so many wonderful people and received so many random complements and sweetest appreciations that I never thought I deserve.

. But my absolute win is my present. I live a life to be thankful for. An amazing set of parents (Ma will be amazing when she allows me chanachur), the best bunch of friends and a wonderfully painted life. I know there are people I can count on no matter what and this assuarance means everything to me. I'm proud of who I am today with all the little wins and big miss-outs. I hope I'm proud of the person I emerge into. I have loved and been loved. This is all life is.

. "Happiness is pretty simple : someone to love, something to do and something to look forward to". - Rita Mae Brown
