Language February 21,2021

richi.writes The 21st of February reminds me of the yesteryears. The tune of "Amar Bhai er rokte rangano ekushe February" on this day always takes me back to the days when I was a kid. I used to wake up very early, wear white and black dresses and go to the Shahid Minar with Baba to offer flowers. That, is one of the most beautiful memories I have of my childhood. On the way Baba always used to explain why this day holds so much importance and why we should always remember the price we had to pay to achieve the language we speak today.... As a kid, I always got goosebumps.

Till this day, I speak Bangla, I respect Bangla. I have kept the practice of reading Bangla even when I got used to English books more. Bangla books is still my first priority, thanks to my parents. It just breaks my heart how Bangla these days has become "unfashionable" to our generation. Teens who listen to Bangla songs are immediately seen as uncool. It's utterly painful to see people thinking it's cooler to not speak Bangla. Despite being born on the soil of the country that's still red with the blood of young Rafiq, Jabbar, Salam who fought for this very language.

English, as important as it is, is still our second language. It's still a language we need to 'learn' in order to master. It's not biologically designed in our DNA. Yet, English is the yardstick to measure education these days. No matter how many times we shout GOOD ENGLISH DOES NOT MEAN GOOD EDUCATION, we will always be judged on our fluency in a language we didn't speak from birth. Personally as an English version student, I have encountered people a bit behind in English being made fun of by other classmates. And the same classmates when they say "Bangla parina" don't realize how betraying this is.

Learn Bangla. Speak Bangla. Read Bangla. Remember, Salam, Rafiq, Shafiq, Barkat, Jabbar did not die on streets in 1952 for some Bangalee student in 2021 to say "Bangla parina" in distorted pronunciation. Speak Bangla like you know nothing but it. Love Bangla more than you love your life, like the language martyrs did. Teach your children Bangla and the history behind it. I hope the children who are putting flowers on Shahid Minar don't do it only for the sake of it. I hope kids today grow up to know history and respect the language they speak. I hope we all touch our hearts today and proudly sing along,
