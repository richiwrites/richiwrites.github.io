She

Life June 12,2020

richi.writes
She once had a young, wandering mind dreaming of a thousand places she'd thrive in

Today she's a 50 year old lady, married off at the age of nineteen

She's labelled as a "housewife", apparently with no "job" to ...serve

Years of putting her children first, then husband, family, she forgot there's a place she too deserves

Years ago, when she was married, this is what her family was told

It's a year long tradition that women don't work in this household

Once she painted her canvases in colors, dreaming of being an artist

Today she forgets to count herself while sorting her priority list

She loved an art of Amrita Shergill called "the little girl in blue"

Her teachers used to say art is what she should pursue

Today She's a mighty warrior, in her kitchen, with a spatula and a knife

She's a scholar, a teacher, teaching her children the lessons of life

Evenings when her husband returns from work, his tea is served just on time

Her husband takes his rest while she makes her children learn their rhymes

She never has a rest time, waking up before everyone, sleeping after everyone's asleep

Her tiredness, her fatigue, visible on her pale skin and even paler lips

One day her son says he wants to marry,

Her eyes immediatley show a sign of worry

As her son says the girl he loves is working

She gets scared, she feels her heart shrinking

When the family says working women are against this household,

Finally in 31 years, she raises her voice bold

She tells her son to marry the girl he wants to

She doesn't want someone else's dreams to be shattered too

She remembers her love for her art and the nights she had sobbed

So finally, she gets her son married to a woman with a job

That night, she brings out her canvas after so many years again

She draws what she loves the most, her family, she paints

Her husband says sorry and asks her if she still loves him the same

She says, "you're the only home I know, my love" and shows him the frame

The frame that holds her art of her husband, children, and this time, she painted herself too

And finally, in 50 years of her life, she feels this world is too good to be true

এই শহর

Poem June 13,2020

richi.writes
এই শহরে ঘুম চেনে না রাত
রাতের শহরও সকালের অপেক্ষায়
নির্ঘুম চোখ কলমে স্বস্তি খোঁজে
জীর্ণ আঙুল কবিতা আউড়ে যায়
নিঃসঙ্গতা ঘোরেফেরে একা... রাতে
চাঁদের চোখেও ঘুম জড়িয়ে আসে
মধ্যরাতের বিষাদ মাখানো চোখ
আদতে শুধুই সকালকে ভালোবাসে

রাত কবিতার পাতায় পাতায় লেখা
ঠিক কতখানি চওড়া হয়েছে ক্ষত
সকাল হলেই লুকোবে রাতের ব্যথা
ব্যস্ত হবে যে যার নিজের মত

গতরাতের কান্না ভেজা চোখে
কাজলের টান গাঢ় থেকে গাঢ়তর
কনসিলারের প্রলেপে ক্লান্ত চোখও
স্বপ্ন দেখার শ্রান্তিতে মরো মরো

পূর্ব আকাশে ভোর হয়ে হয়ে এলে
সূর্যও যখন আড়মোড়া ভেঙে ওঠে
লাল লিপস্টিক আদরে লুকিয়ে রাখে
ক্লান্তির ছাপ বিবর্ণ কোনো ঠোঁটে
পরিপাটি সাজে গুছিয়ে নেওয়ার পরে

নাকের ওপর চশমাটা ঠেকে শেষে
আবার একটা নতুন সকাল এলেই
আঘাত লুকোনো মিথ্যে ছদ্মবেশে

এই শহরের সকালগুলো দুঃখ চেনে না
এই শহরের সকালগুলোর বড্ড তাড়া
এই শহরে রাত বাড়লেই কফি কাপে ঠোঁট
এই শহরটা মধ্যরাতে দিশেহারা

কংক্রিটের দালানগুলোর ছোট্ট ঘরে
কান্নাগুলো আশ্রয় খোঁজে রাতের কাছে
এই শহরের নিঃশব্দ বিষন্নতা
মধ্যরাতের প্রহরগুলোয় অাজও বাঁচে

এই শহরটা ঘুম চেনে না রাতে
রাত জাগা তার শিরায় শিরায় মিশে
আঘাত লুকিয়ে ব্যস্ততা গায়ে মাখা
শহুরে মনের চিরকালীন অভ্যেসে

এ্যালার্ম ক্লকের বিদঘুটে ক্রিং ক্রিং এ
আবার একটা রাত শেষ হয়ে আসে
এ শহর মাতে পরিচিত ব্যস্ততায়
দীর্ঘ রজনী সমাপ্তির অবকাশে

Sushant Singh Rajput

R.I.P June 17,2020

richi.writes
It took me a lot to bring myself to write about the death of Sushant Singh Rajput. He was one of my favourites. He still is and I believe he always will be. It breaks my heart to think that death seemed easier to him than living a life of name and fame. We can see what Bollywood nepotism did to Sushant. His talent, his worth went unrecognized because of the mockeries and fakeness of this show business industry leaving us with a conclusion that bollywood never really deserved him.
... I realize all religions are strongly against suicide but all religions also teach us to sympathize people, to be kind. 2020 is the worst year possible but on the contrary, this year is also continuously teaching us to be kind and generous and humane. My mother has worked with the telecounseling program "Moner jotno mobile e" and she told me that people going through depression won't immediately think of a helpline number. It's amazing to offer listening ears to random people but first, we be there for our friends. We listen to our closest ones. Please let's not get annoyed when our friends talk about their same problems over and over again, let's not address their issues as "drama", let's not ignore when someone has suicidal thoughts. What can we do? If we're at a better place than someone else, we don't play the power card. If we are lucky enough to be in a stable mental state, we need to try our best to make our struggling friends feel worthy and loved rather than just saying "you'll heal" or "suicide is for losers". We don't "fix" them this way. We need to take them for professional help, arrange helplines or counselling.

I hope we understand how our words or actions can effect others. I hope we never make anyone feel left out because of our conscious judgemental attitudes. I hope we believe in our friends and can make ourselves a safe place for them to approach. Because noone deserves to think they no longer deserve to live. I hope Sushant is finally at a far better place than this world that continuously failed him. We're all sending you love, Sushant

Baba

Love June 21,2020

richi.writes
Dear Baba,
If I'm asked about my favourite memory of childhood,
I'll say when I was very little and I loved watching moving vehicles from up above
So some afternoons, you took me out and stood beside me on the overbridge and we looked at those vehicles for long
Or ...maybe when I stopped crying the moment you took me in your arms, you sang me to sleep while walking on the rooftop at nights

Thank you Baba

For being the first lap I've ever known
For letting your chest be an escape while Ma scolded me
For sending me pictures in silly filters
For making me laugh while I cry
For wiping my tears with your hands that felt like magic wands
For recharging my phone when I ran out of balance
For being my safe space
For putting up with my ranting sessions and endless conspiracies against your wife
I could see the pride in your eyes when I got selected for the school show
I could also see in your eyes that you believed in me even when I failed an exam
Thank you Baba, for loving me while I succeed
And for loving me more while I failed

. And sometimes I wish you didn't live so far away in another city
Sometimes I wish you came home every evening like the dads of my friends
Sometimes I wish you could watch me grow up
But I love this, Baba
I love being all excited to open the door when you're home before anyone else does
I love waiting for school to be over on the days you come to pick me up
And Baba I'm sorry I fail you sometimes
I know on some days I'm not the daughter you deserve
And I'm the best you could find
Who else would love you like I do?
Sometimes I love that I have no sibling so nobody else will ever call you "Baba"

And I know I don't say it often
But you're my favourite human to exist
You're my pride, my strength, my shelter
The man I look up to
The man who has answers to all my questions
Hold my hand, Baba, because I'm scared of the life I'm yet to face
Hold my hand like you do while I cross the roads
Be with me Baba, like you've always been

When I'm asked what I want to be, I say I want to be exactly like you
And see, Baba, just like you're in my life, you're the hero in my poetries too
